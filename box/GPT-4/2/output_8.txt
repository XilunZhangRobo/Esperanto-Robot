Plan:

1. Open the gripper.
2. Get the graspable point of cubeA.
3. Move to the position above cubeA.
4. Move down to the graspable point of cubeA.
5. Close the gripper to grasp cubeA.
6. Lift cubeA up.
7. Get the top point of cubeB.
8. Move to the position above cubeB.
9. Move down to the top point of cubeB to stack cubeA on top of cubeB.
10. Open the gripper to release cubeA.

Python Code:

```python
import numpy as np

# Step 1: Open the gripper
open_gripper()

# Step 2: Get the graspable point of cubeA
cubeA_graspable_point = get_graspable_point('cubeA')

# Step 3: Move to the position above cubeA
move_to_position(cubeA_graspable_point + np.array([0, 0, get_size('cubeA')[2]]))

# Step 4: Move down to the graspable point of cubeA
move_to_position(cubeA_graspable_point)

# Step 5: Close the gripper to grasp cubeA
close_gripper()

# Step 6: Lift cubeA up
move_to_position(cubeA_graspable_point + np.array([0, 0, get_size('cubeA')[2]]))

# Step 7: Get the top point of cubeB
cubeB_top_point = get_graspable_point('cubeB') + np.array([0, 0, get_size('cubeB')[2]])

# Step 8: Move to the position above cubeB
move_to_position(cubeB_top_point + np.array([0, 0, get_size('cubeA')[2]]))

# Step 9: Move down to the top point of cubeB to stack cubeA on top of cubeB
move_to_position(cubeB_top_point)

# Step 10: Open the gripper to release cubeA
open_gripper()
```

This code assumes that the get_graspable_point function returns the center of the bottom face of the cube, and the get_size function returns the size of the cube in the format [length, width, height]. The np.array([0, 0, get_size('cubeA')[2]]) is used to move the gripper up and down by the height of the cube.
