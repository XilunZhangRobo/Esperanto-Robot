Plan:

1. Open the gripper.
2. Get the graspable point of cubeA.
3. Move to the position above cubeA.
4. Move down to grasp cubeA.
5. Close the gripper to grasp cubeA.
6. Lift cubeA up.
7. Move to the position above cubeB.
8. Move down to place cubeA on cubeB.
9. Open the gripper to release cubeA.
10. Move up to avoid hitting cubeA.
11. Get the graspable point of ballA.
12. Move to the position above ballA.
13. Move down to grasp ballA.
14. Close the gripper to grasp ballA.
15. Lift ballA up.
16. Move to the position above cubeA (which is now on top of cubeB).
17. Move down to place ballA on cubeA.
18. Open the gripper to release ballA.

Python Code:

```python
import numpy as np

# Step 1
open_gripper()

# Step 2
cubeA_graspable_point = get_graspable_point('cubeA')

# Step 3
move_to_position(np.array([cubeA_graspable_point[0], cubeA_graspable_point[1], cubeA_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeA')[2]/2]))

# Step 4
move_to_position(cubeA_graspable_point)

# Step 5
close_gripper()

# Step 6
move_to_position(np.array([cubeA_graspable_point[0], cubeA_graspable_point[1], cubeA_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeA')[2]/2]))

# Step 7
cubeB_graspable_point = get_graspable_point('cubeB')
move_to_position(np.array([cubeB_graspable_point[0], cubeB_graspable_point[1], cubeB_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeB')[2]/2 + get_size('cubeA')[2]/2]))

# Step 8
move_to_position(np.array([cubeB_graspable_point[0], cubeB_graspable_point[1], cubeB_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeB')[2]/2]))

# Step 9
open_gripper()

# Step 10
move_to_position(np.array([cubeB_graspable_point[0], cubeB_graspable_point[1], cubeB_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeB')[2]/2 + get_size('cubeA')[2]/2]))

# Step 11
ballA_graspable_point = get_graspable_point('ballA')

# Step 12
move_to_position(np.array([ballA_graspable_point[0], ballA_graspable_point[1], ballA_graspable_point[2] + get_size('ballA')[2]/2]))

# Step 13
move_to_position(ballA_graspable_point)

# Step 14
close_gripper()

# Step 15
move_to_position(np.array([ballA_graspable_point[0], ballA_graspable_point[1], ballA_graspable_point[2] + get_size('ballA')[2]/2]))

# Step 16
move_to_position(np.array([cubeB_graspable_point[0], cubeB_graspable_point[1], cubeB_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeB')[2]/2 + get_size('cubeA')[2] + get_size('ballA')[2]/2]))

# Step 17
move_to_position(np.array([cubeB_graspable_point[0], cubeB_graspable_point[1], cubeB_graspable_point[2] + get_size('cubeB')[2]/2 + get_size('cubeA')[2])))

# Step 18
open_gripper()
```
Please note that the above code assumes that the z-coordinate of the graspable point is at the bottom of the object. If it's at the center, you would need to adjust the z-coordinate calculations accordingly.
